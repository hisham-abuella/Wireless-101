Operations

Adding Vectors

Combine forces, velocities, and directions by adding vectors component by component. The tip-to-tail rule gives a beautiful geometric picture.

Tip-to-tail rule
Place the tail of the second vector at the tip of the first. The resultant vector goes from the start of the first to the end of the second.

Geometry

Transformations

Matrices encode geometric operations. Every transformation preserves the grid lines as parallel and evenly spaced.

  • Rotation — spin space around the origin
  • Scaling — stretch or compress along axes
  • Reflection — mirror across a line
  • Shearing — slant space in one direction

History

A Brief History

Linear algebra evolved over centuries, shaped by mathematicians seeking to solve systems of equations and understand geometry.

1693 — Leibniz introduces determinants for solving linear systems
1750 — Cramer publishes his rule for solving equations
1844 — Grassmann invents the algebra of vector spaces
1855 — Cayley formalizes matrix algebra

Application

Google PageRank

Linear algebra ranks every webpage on the internet. Google’s PageRank algorithm models the web as a giant matrix of links and finds the dominant eigenvector — the steady-state importance of every page.

The key idea
A page is important if important pages link to it — a recursive definition solved by linear algebra

Impact

LA Everywhere

Linear algebra is the universal language of applied mathematics. It appears wherever data, space, or systems interact.

  • Computer graphics — rendering, animation, shaders
  • Machine learning — neural networks are matrix operations
  • Physics — quantum mechanics lives in vector spaces
  • Engineering — circuit analysis, structural modeling
  • Data science — PCA, regression, dimensionality reduction
