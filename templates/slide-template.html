What are Electromagnetic Waves?

Oscillating electric and magnetic fields that travel through space at the speed of light. They don't need a medium — they work in a vacuum.

The EM Spectrum

Different frequency ranges serve different purposes:

  • Radio (3 kHz–300 GHz) — Broadcasting, WiFi, cellular
  • Microwave (1–300 GHz) — Radar, satellite, 5G
  • Infrared — Remote controls, thermal imaging
  • Visible light — What our eyes detect
  • UV, X-ray, Gamma — Medical, scientific

How Information Rides a Wave

A carrier wave alone carries no data. Modulation encodes information by varying the wave's amplitude, frequency, or phase.

Three ways to modulate
AM → vary amplitude
FM → vary frequency
PM → vary phase
